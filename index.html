Personal Arrangements Checklist

Phase 1

Travel & Support

Essential legal, travel, and financial arrangements for your journey:

  • Visa Requirement: International students travelling to Shanghai will require a valid visa, which will be arranged centrally by HKU.
  • Flight Booking: Students are responsible for booking their own flight arrangements.
  • Insurance: Insurance coverage will be provided by the School during your stay in Shanghai.
  • Financial Support: Students will receive a travel subsidy of RMB 3,000 and a living allowance of RMB 12,000 upon successful completion of the Programme.
💡 Note: A comprehensive Visa Application Guideline will be sent to your email shortly. Please prepare your documents accordingly.
Phase 2

Communication & Finance

Essential setups required for daily life and digital payment ecosystems:

  • Mainland Mobile Number: A Mainland mobile number is required for bank account opening and daily communication. If you do not have one, please purchase a SIM card in HK before departure.
  • Bank Account: A Mainland bank account is required for daily expenses and allowance disbursement. You can fulfill this via one of the following options:
    • Option A (Independent): Travel to Mainland China and open an account independently before 30 June (any bank is acceptable).
    • Option B (On-campus): Open it on campus during Orientation Day via ICBC on-site counter services.
  • WeChat Account: Please set up a WeChat account prior to arrival. A WeChat group will be created for communication purposes.
Phase 3

Campus Logistics

Finalizing your move and ensuring seamless access to the Shanghai campus:

  • Dormitory Check-in: Officially opens on 29 August 2026. Room allocations are randomized by the Campus Service Office.
  • Accommodation Details: 2-student shared apartments, featuring individual private bedrooms.
  • Campus Access: You must submit a photograph in advance to activate secure building access.

Action Required

To facilitate administrative arrangements, please complete the online form via 2026-2027 Shanghai Semester - Student Information Collection by 30 June 2026 (Tuesday). All personal data collected will be used solely for purposes related to insurance, visa, and accommodation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do we need to pay the tuition fee in the Semester?
A: The tuition fee for Semester 1 of 2026/27 will remain the same, regardless of whether you study at the HKU campus or at the HKU CDS Teaching & Research Site in Shanghai.
Q: What is the amount of learning subsidy?
A: Participating students will receive a travel subsidy of RMB 3,000 and a living allowance of RMB 12,000 upon successful completion of the semester in Shanghai.
Q: Will the academic calendar align with the holiday system observed in Mainland China, or will it follow Hong Kong's schedule instead?
A: The academic calendar will follow that of HKU and align with HKSAR public holidays.
Q: Do students still select the courses via portal, or is there a separate place to select?
A: Students can enroll in the courses via the SIS portal, similar to the enrollment arrangement for other courses offered in HK.
Q: As the courses are taught in blocks, will the final exam be all held at the end of semester or end of block, and will the paper be the same as the Hong Kong campus?
A: Examinations will be held at the end of the semester, concurrently with the Hong Kong examination period. The School will adopt the same examination procedure for courses offered in both Hong Kong and Shanghai.
Q: Do we need to pay the dormitory fee in the Semester?
A: Free student dormitory (double occupancy in a 2-bedroom apartment) will be provided in Shanghai during your study there.
Q: Regarding the student dormitories, is there a tentative date for when they will be open for us?
A: Dormitory check-in will commence on 29 August 2026.
Q: How far is the student dormitory from the actual teaching site in Shanghai (e.g., Zhangjiang Hi-Tech Park), and is there a shuttle bus or convenient public transport?
A: The student dormitory is approximately a 20-minute walking distance from the teaching site, and a shuttle bus will be provided.
Q: Can I choose my roommate for the accommodation in Shanghai?
A: Room allocations, including apartment pairings, are managed randomly by the Campus Service Office to foster integration.
Q: What should I do if I cannot open a mainland bank account before 30 June?
A: Don't worry. If you are unable to open one in advance, you can fully rely on the on-campus counter services provided by ICBC during the official Orientation Day.
Q: Is attendance mandatory for the Reading Week activities?
A: No, corporate visits and career development talks organized during the Reading Week are completely optional. You are free to plan your personal travel or study schedule during this period.
